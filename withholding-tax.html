Overview

What is Withholding Tax?

Withholding tax is tax deducted at source from various payments including dividends, interest, rent, professional fees, management fees and other specified payments. It must be remitted to KRA by the 20th of the following month.

Who needs this service

Is Withholding Tax filing right for you?

Businesses Making Payments

Companies Paying Dividends

Companies distributing dividends must withhold tax on dividend payments to shareholders.

Businesses with Professional Services

If you pay professional fees, management fees or consultancy fees, withholding tax applies.

Landlords and Tenants

FAQ

Common questions about withholding tax.

When is withholding tax due?

Withholding tax must be filed and remitted to KRA by the 20th of the following month after the deduction.

Who should deduct withholding tax?

Any person or business making payments subject to withholding tax must deduct the tax and remit it to KRA.

What happens if I don't deduct withholding tax?

Failure to deduct and remit withholding tax can result in penalties, interest charges and legal consequences.

What is a withholding tax certificate?

A withholding tax certificate is issued to the payee confirming that tax has been deducted and remitted to KRA.
