Understanding taxes

Tax types explained for Kenyan businesses.

Personal Income Tax

Tax on income earned by individuals from employment, business, investments and other sources. Rates range from 10% to 35%.

PAYE (Pay As You Earn)

Tax deducted from employee salaries by employers. Calculated based on monthly income and remitted to KRA by the 9th of each month.

Turnover Tax

Simplified tax for small businesses with annual turnover below KES 5 million. Calculated at 3% of gross sales.

VAT (Value Added Tax)

Tax on goods and services at 16%. Businesses registered for VAT must file monthly returns by the 20th.

Rental Income Tax

Tax on rental income from property. Calculated at 10% of gross rental income after allowable deductions.

FAQ

Common questions about tax calculations.

How accurate is this tax calculator?

This calculator provides an estimate based on the information you provide and current tax rates. For official filings, consult a tax professional or KRA directly.

What tax deductions can I claim?

Common deductions include mortgage interest, insurance premiums, pension contributions, charitable donations and business expenses. A tax professional can help you maximize deductions.

When are tax payments due?

Tax due dates vary: PAYE by 9th, VAT by 20th, Income Tax by June 30th. Our team can help you track deadlines and ensure timely filing.

Do I need to pay tax if I'm self-employed?

Yes, self-employed individuals must file and pay income tax based on their business income. We can help you understand your obligations.

What happens if I don't pay taxes?

Failure to pay taxes can result in penalties, interest charges, and potential legal action from KRA. We help ensure you stay compliant.
