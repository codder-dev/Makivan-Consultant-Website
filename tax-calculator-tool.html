Understanding your taxes

What each deduction means for you.

NSSF

6% of pensionable pay. Tier I up to 8,000, Tier II up to 18,000. Your retirement savings contribution.

SHA

2.75% of gross salary. Replaced NHIF for health insurance coverage.

Housing Levy

1.5% of gross salary. Contributes to the Affordable Housing Program.

PAYE

Progressive tax on taxable pay. Rates from 10% to 35% based on income brackets.

FAQ

Common questions about tax calculations.

What is the difference between Gross Pay and Basic Pay?

Gross Pay includes all earnings (basic salary + allowances + bonuses). Basic Pay is the base salary before any allowances or benefits.

What are the NSSF Tier options?

Tier I: 6% up to KES 8,000. Tier I & II: 6% up to KES 18,000. Old Rates: 5% up to KES 400. Custom: Enter your own NSSF amount.

What is SHA?

SHA (Social Health Insurance Fund) replaced NHIF. It's 2.75% of gross salary for health insurance coverage.

What is the Housing Levy?

The Housing Levy is 1.5% of gross salary contributed to the Affordable Housing Program.

What is the personal relief?

Personal relief is KES 2,400 per month (KES 28,800 per year) deducted from your gross PAYE to reduce your tax burden.
