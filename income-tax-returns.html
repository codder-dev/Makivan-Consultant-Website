Overview

What is Income Tax Returns Filing?

Income tax returns filing is an annual obligation for all individuals and companies earning income in Kenya. It involves declaring your income, calculating tax payable, and submitting the returns to KRA by June 30th each year.

Who needs this service

Is Income Tax Returns Filing right for you?

Individual Taxpayers

Every individual earning income in Kenya must file annual income tax returns regardless of employment status.

Companies & Corporations

All registered companies and corporations must file annual income tax returns with KRA.

Self-Employed Individuals

Freelancers, consultants and self-employed individuals must file income tax returns on their earnings.

Business Owners

Business owners must file income tax returns on business profits and personal income.

FAQ

Common questions about income tax returns.

When is the income tax returns deadline?

Income tax returns for individuals and companies must be filed by June 30th each year.

What happens if I don't file my income tax returns?

Failure to file income tax returns can result in penalties, interest charges and potential legal consequences.

Do I need to file if I'm employed?

Yes, even if your taxes are deducted through PAYE, you still need to file annual income tax returns to declare all your income sources.

What deductions can I claim?

Deductions include mortgage interest, insurance premiums, pension contributions, charitable donations and business expenses.
