Understanding KRA documents

What you need to know about KRA registration documents.

KRA PIN

What it is: Your personal or business identification number for all KRA transactions. It's required for filing returns, paying taxes and conducting business in Kenya.

Who needs it: Every individual and business earning income in Kenya.

Required

CR12

What it is: Certificate of directors and shareholders showing company ownership structure. It's required for banking, tenders and compliance.

Who needs it: All registered companies in Kenya.

For Companies

CR13

What it is: Certificate of compliance confirming your company has met its filing obligations with the Registrar.

Who needs it: Companies that have filed their annual returns.

For Companies

CR7

What it is: Annual returns filing certificate confirming compliance with the Registrar of Companies.

Who needs it: Companies that have filed their annual returns.

For Companies

FAQ

Common questions about KRA PIN services.

How do I get a KRA PIN?

You can apply for a KRA PIN online through the iTax system. We can help you with the entire application process to ensure it's done correctly.

How long does it take to get a KRA PIN?

PIN applications are typically processed within 1-3 business days if all documents are correctly submitted. We help expedite the process.

What is a CR12 document?

CR12 is a certificate from the Registrar of Companies showing the current directors and shareholders of a company. It's required for banking, tenders and compliance.

How do I update my KRA contact information?

You can update your contact details through the iTax system. We can help you make these updates correctly and promptly.

What if I've lost my KRA PIN certificate?

We can help you obtain a replacement PIN certificate from KRA. The process is straightforward with proper documentation.

Can I use my KRA PIN for multiple businesses?

No, each business entity requires its own KRA PIN. However, individuals can have one personal PIN for multiple income sources.
